# TopRanker Core Values v1

## 1. Restaurants first. Proof before expansion.
TopRanker's current mission is singular: prove the core loop works for restaurants in Dallas.

Everything else is secondary.

We do not let future categories, platform fantasies, or long-term architecture distract us from the first real proof:
rate -> consequence -> ranking

Restaurants are the testing ground.
Restaurants prove execution.
Everything else waits until the restaurant loop is undeniably working.

---

## 2. We build truth, not noise.
TopRanker exists because trust is broken.

Generic reviews are noisy.
Search results are cluttered.
Fake sentiment, bought reviews, and weak incentives distort reality.

We are building a system that is:
- clearer
- fairer
- more structured
- more accountable
- harder to game

We do not add noise.
We reduce it.

---

## 3. Structured scoring beats vague reviews.
We do not believe long-form reviews are the core product.

The core input is fast, structured scoring.
Users answer a small set of meaningful questions.
Those answers create real sub-scores and change live rankings.

Optional text can help.
But essays are not the backbone.
Math, consequence, and clarity are the backbone.

---

## 4. Every rating must have consequence.
A rating should never feel like a pebble dropped into the ocean.

When a user contributes, something should happen:
- a score strengthens
- confidence improves
- a challenger gap narrows
- a rank moves
- a category becomes more trustworthy

If a contribution has no visible consequence, the product becomes forgettable.

Consequence is one of our strongest differentiators.
We protect it.

---

## 5. Credibility is contextual and earned.
Not every contribution should count equally.

But we do not score people as human beings.
We measure credibility in context.

A contributor earns more influence through:
- consistency
- quality participation
- low-flag behavior
- sustained contribution
- trustworthy patterns over time

We do not build universal moral scoring.
We build contextual credibility that improves rankings.

---

## 6. Transparency matters, but gaming must stay hard.
People deserve to understand what affects rankings and credibility.

Users should know:
- why something changed
- what improves credibility
- what hurts credibility
- how rankings are formed
- how they can grow their influence

But we do not reveal anti-gaming internals, exact abuse thresholds, or exploit paths.

Our principle:
clear box, locked engine room.

---

## 7. Ranking businesses fairly is socially valuable.
We are not here to protect bad performance from accountability.

If a business consistently delivers:
- poor quality
- poor service
- weak value
- low return intent

it should rank lower.

That is not cruelty.
That is honest market feedback.

Our responsibility is to make rankings:
- fair
- transparent
- hard to game
- honest about low-confidence states
- recoverable through real improvement

A lower rank is not a permanent scar.
Rankings stay live so businesses can improve and rise again.

Our system is not punishment.
It is accountability with mobility.

The social good of TopRanker is:
- helping customers waste less money
- rewarding businesses that genuinely care
- exposing businesses that survive on noise instead of quality
- giving smaller good businesses a real chance to rise

We earn the right to rank only if the system stays fair, humble, explainable, and reversible through better performance.

---

## 8. We do not confuse confidence with precision.
If data is weak, the product must say so.

We do not present tiny sample sizes with fake certainty.
We use:
- provisional ranking
- early ranking
- low confidence
- needs more ratings

Low-data honesty is not optional.
It is central to trust.

---

## 9. New users must feel progress, not powerlessness.
We tell the truth about credibility and influence.
But we do not make new contributors feel small, useless, or humiliated.

A new user should feel:
- welcomed
- counted
- on a path
- motivated to grow credibility

We frame growth as progress, not punishment.

---

## 10. Premium, clear, alive.
TopRanker should feel:
- premium
- clear
- fair
- alive
- consequential

It should not feel:
- gimmicky
- over-gamified
- fake-precise
- like a prettier Yelp clone
- like a badge economy pretending to be a trust product

Our visual quality matters because trust products cannot feel sloppy.
But polish must never outrun product truth.

---

## 11. The core loop comes before meta-systems.
Badges, referrals, rewards, settings, legal surfaces, dashboards, and admin tools are all secondary.

If they strengthen the core loop, they are useful.
If they distract from it, they are debt.

We prioritize:
- rating flow
- consequence visibility
- ranking clarity
- credibility fairness
- confidence honesty

before we prioritize:
- growth loops
- reward ladders
- decorative progression systems
- non-core expansion

---

## 12. TopRanker is our brand. Best In is our format.
TopRanker is our house brand.
Best In is our product format.

TopRanker represents the larger system:
- live rankings
- credibility-weighted contribution
- structured scoring
- accountability through movement

Best In represents how users experience that system:
- Best in Dallas
- Best biryani in Irving
- Best fade in Plano
- Best cold brew in the city

We build under TopRanker and speak through Best In.

This keeps us broad in architecture and simple in experience.
We want the system to scale, but the product to feel instantly understandable.

---

## 13. One source of truth.
Docs, code, sprint notes, audits, and product copy must agree.

Stale docs are bugs.
Contradictions are not cosmetic.
If README, architecture, tier semantics, and code disagree, trust erodes inside the team before it erodes outside it.

We do not tolerate drift as normal.

---

## 14. Honest retros, not morale theater.
Retros exist to reveal truth, not preserve vibes.

Every retro must say:
- what actually improved
- what was overbuilt
- what remained broken
- what gets fixed next
- what is deferred or dropped

Action items must always be:
- DONE
- DEFERRED
- DROPPED
- OPEN with clear ownership

Nothing lives forever in vague backlog purgatory.

---

## 15. Expansion is earned, not assumed.
The platform may generalize.
That does not mean expansion is cheap.

Every new category is not just a schema problem.
It is a:
- behavior problem
- trust problem
- attribution problem
- moderation problem
- legal problem
- cold-start problem

We do not expand because the architecture can.
We expand only when the first category is healthy and the next category passes real readiness gates.

---

## 16. Build narrower than ambition.
We are allowed to dream big.
We are not allowed to execute vaguely.

The long-term platform can be broad.
The current product must stay narrow enough to become undeniable.

Our rule:
build a brand that can expand,
but build a product that can win one wedge first.

---

## 17. We choose clarity over cleverness.
We do not choose names, flows, or systems because they sound impressive.

We choose what makes the product:
- instantly understandable
- emotionally clear
- naturally usable
- easy to trust

If users cannot quickly understand what the product is doing and why it matters, we have failed.

---

## 18. We earn trust through humility.
A ranking system is not morally right just because it exists.

We earn legitimacy by being:
- fair
- transparent
- honest about uncertainty
- careful with confidence
- open to improvement
- resistant to gaming
- recoverable through better performance

Humility is part of the product.

---

## 19. We do not build for applause.
We do not optimize for:
- impressive sprint counts
- beautiful process theater
- feature volume
- internal excitement
- documentation that outruns reality

We optimize for:
- real user value
- real trust
- real contribution
- real return behavior
- real ranking usefulness

The product is the truth.
Everything else supports it.

---

## 20. Our final operating principle
When in doubt, choose the path that strengthens:

rate -> consequence -> ranking

and reduces drift.
